Aloo Paratha

Ingredients :-
For Dough
  • 2 cups Whole Wheat Flour (Atta)
  • Water as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp Oil or Ghee
For Potato Filling
  • 3 large Potatoes, boiled and mashed (Aloo)
  • 1 large Onion, finely chopped (Pyaz)
  • 2 Green Chilies, finely chopped (Hari Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Ginger, grated (Adrak)
  • 1/2 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch) -
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves, chopped (Hara Dhaniya)
  • 1 tbsp Lemon Juice (Nimbu)
For Cooking
  • Ghee or Oil for cooking
Recipe :-
Step 1: Prepare the Dough
  1. In a large bowl, mix whole wheat flour and salt.
  2. Add water gradually and knead to form a smooth and soft dough.
  3. Add 1 tbsp oil or ghee and knead again until the dough is pliable.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.
Step 2: Prepare the Potato Filling
  1. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add grated ginger and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Mix well.
  6. Add boiled and mashed potatoes. Mix thoroughly.
  7. Add salt to taste and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add chopped coriander leaves and lemon juice. Mix well and cook for another minute.
  9. Remove from heat and let the mixture cool.
Step 3: Stuff and Roll the Parathas
  1. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
  2. Roll out one dough ball into a small circle.
  3. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center.
  4. Bring the edges of the dough together to seal the filling inside.
  5. Gently flatten the stuffed dough ball and roll it out into a circle, about 6-7 inches in diameter.
Step 4: Cook the Parathas
  1. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat.
  2. Place the rolled-out paratha on the hot tawa.
  3. Cook until small bubbles form on the surface, then flip it.
  4. Apply ghee or oil on the cooked side, flip again, and apply ghee or oil on the other side.
  5. Cook until both sides are golden brown and crispy.
  6. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
Step 5: Finish and Serve
  1. Serve hot aloo parathas with yogurt (dahi), pickle (achar), or any chutney of your choice.
Enjoy your delicious Aloo Parathas!
