Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients :-
For Paneer
  • 250 g Paneer, cut into cubes
  • 2 tbsp Butter
  • 2 tbsp Oil
For Gravy
  • 2 large Onions, finely chopped (Pyaz)
  • 3 large Tomatoes, pureed (Tamatar)
  • 2-3 Green Chilies, slit (Hari Mirch)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Powder (Jeera Powder)
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Cream (Malai)
  • 1/2 cup Milk
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 2 tbsp Cashew Nuts (Kaju), soaked and blended into a paste
  • 2 tbsp Kasuri Methi (Dried Fenugreek Leaves)
  • Salt to taste
For Garnish
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves (Hara Dhaniya)
Recipe :-
Step 1: Prepare the Paneer
  1. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Lightly fry the paneer cubes until they are golden brown. Remove and set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Gravy
  1. In the same pan, heat 2 tbsp butter.
  2. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
Step 3: Add Spices and Cashew Paste
  1. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well.
  2. Add the cashew paste and cook for 2-3 minutes.
Step 4: Cook the Gravy
  1. Add milk, water, and salt. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  2. Add the fresh cream and kasuri methi. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
Step 5: Add Paneer
  1. Add the fried paneer cubes to the gravy. Mix gently to coat the paneer with the masala.
  2. Cook for 5-7 minutes on low heat until the paneer absorbs the flavors
Step 6: Garnish and Serve
  1. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves and a dollop of butter.
  2. erve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
Enjoy your delicious Paneer Butter Masala!
