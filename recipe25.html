Sambar

Ingredients :-
For Sambar
  • 1 cup Toor Dal (Pigeon Pea Lentils)
  • 3 cups Water
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • Salt to taste
For Vegetables
  • 1 Carrot, diced (Gajar)
  • 1 Potato, diced (Aloo)
  • 1 Drumstick, cut into pieces (Sahjan)
  • 1 Tomato, chopped (Tamatar)
  • 6-8 Pearl Onions (Sambar Onions)
  • 1 Brinjal, diced (Baingan)
  • 1/2 cup Pumpkin, diced (Kaddu)
  • 5-6 Ladyfingers, cut into pieces (Bhindi)
For Tamarind Paste
  • 1 tbsp Tamarind Pulp (Imli)
  • 1/2 cup Water
For Sambar Masala
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard Seeds (Rai)
  • 1/2 tsp Fenugreek Seeds (Methi)
  • 2 Dry Red Chilies
  • 10-12 Curry Leaves (Kadi Patta)
  • 1/4 tsp Asafoetida (Hing)
  • 2 tbsp Sambar Powder
For Garnish
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves, chopped (Hara Dhaniya)
Recipe :-
Step 1: Cook the Dal
  1. Wash the toor dal thoroughly and soak it for 30 minutes.
  2. In a pressure cooker, add the soaked dal, 3 cups of water, turmeric powder, and salt.
  3. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles until the dal is soft and cooked. Mash the dal and set aside.
Step 2: Prepare Tamarind Paste
  1. Soak tamarind pulp in 1/2 cup warm water for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Extract the tamarind juice by squeezing the pulp. Strain and set aside.
Step 3: Cook the Vegetables
  1. In a large pot, add diced carrot, potato, drumstick, pearl onions, brinjal, pumpkin, ladyfingers, and chopped tomato.
  2. Add enough water to cover the vegetables and cook until they are tender.
Step 4: Prepare the Sambar Masala
  1. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add fenugreek seeds, dry red chilies, curry leaves, and asafoetida. Saut√© for a minute.
  4. Add sambar powder and mix well.
Step 5: Combine Dal, Vegetables, and Masala
  1. Add the mashed dal to the cooked vegetables.
  2. Add the tamarind paste and mix well.
  3. Add the prepared sambar masala to the dal and vegetable mixture.
  4. Bring to a boil and then simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld togethe
Step 6: Finish and Serve
  1. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  2. Serve hot with steamed rice, idli, dosa, or vada.
Enjoy your delicious Sambar!
