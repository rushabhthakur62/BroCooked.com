Veg Biryani

Ingredients :-
For Rice
  • 1.5 cups Basmati Rice
  • 3 cups Water
  • 1 Bay Leaf (Tej Patta)
  • 4-5 Cloves (Laung)
  • 2-3 Green Cardamom (Elaichi)
  • 1-inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini)
  • Salt to taste
For Vegetables
  • 2 tbsp Oi
  • 1 large Onion, thinly sliced (Pyaz)
  • 1 cup Mixed Vegetables (Carrot, Peas, Beans, Potato), chopped
  • 1 large Tomato, chopped (Tamatar)
  • 2-3 Green Chilies, slit (Hari Mirch)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • 1/2 cup Yogurt (Dahi)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Powder (Jeera Powder)
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1/4 cup Fresh Coriander Leaves, chopped (Hara Dhaniya)
  • 1/4 cup Fresh Mint Leaves, chopped (Pudina)
  • Salt to taste
For Layering
  • 1 tbsp Ghee (optional)
  • A pinch of Saffron (Kesar) soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk
  • 1/4 cup Fried Onions (Birista)
Recipe :-
Step 1: Prepare the Rice
  1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly in cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a large pot, bring 3 cups of water to a boil. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon stick, and salt.
  4. Add the soaked rice and cook until it is 70% done (the grains should still have a bite to them). Drain and set aside.
Step 2: Cook the Vegetables
  1. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add thinly sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté until the raw smell disappears
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well.
  6. Add the mixed vegetables and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add yogurt, chopped coriander leaves, and mint leaves. Mix well and cook until the vegetables are tender.
Step 3: Layer the Biryani
  1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread a layer of the partially cooked rice.
  2. Add a layer of the cooked vegetables on top of the rice
  3. Repeat the layers until all the rice and vegetables are used up, finishing with a layer of rice on top.
  4. Drizzle the saffron milk and ghee (if using) over the top layer of rice.
  5. Sprinkle fried onions (birista) on top.
Step 4: Cook the Biryani
  1. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid to trap the steam.
  2. Cook on low heat (dum) for 20-25 minutes, ensuring the rice and vegetables absorb all the flavors.
Step 5: Finish and Serve
  1. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix the layers before serving.
  2. Serve hot with raita, salad, or a side of your choice.
Enjoy your delicious Vegetable Biryani!
