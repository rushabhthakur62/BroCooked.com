Chicken Korma

Ingredients :-
For Marination
  • 1 kg Chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup Yogurt (Dahi)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • Salt to taste
For Gravy
  • 2 large Onions, finely sliced (Pyaz)
  • 1 cup Cashew Nuts (Kaju), soaked in water
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Cream (Malai)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • 2-3 Green Chilies, slit (Hari Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1/2 tsp Nutmeg Powder (Jaiphal Powder)
  • 1/2 cup Ghee or Oil
  • 2-3 tbsp Freshly Chopped Coriander Leaves (Hara Dhaniya)
  • Salt to taste
Recipe :-
Step 1: Marinate the Chicken
  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  2. Add chicken pieces and coat well with the marinade. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
Step 2: Prepare the Gravy
  1. Grind soaked cashew nuts into a smooth paste using little water.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pan over medium heat.
  3. Add finely sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and nutmeg powder. Sauté for another minute.
  6. Add the marinated chicken along with any marinade left in the bowl. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the chicken changes color.
Step 3: Cook the Korma
  1. Add cashew paste and mix well. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add enough water to cover the chicken pieces. Bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 20-25 minutes or until the chicken is tender and cooked through.
  4. Stir in fresh cream and simmer for another 2-3 minutes.Adjust salt and consistency of the gravy as per your preference.
Step 4: Garnish and Serve
  1. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  2. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
Enjoy your delicious Chicken Korma!
