Pindi Chole

Ingredients :-
For Chole
  • 1.5 cups Chickpeas (Chole), soaked overnight
  • 4-5 cups Water
  • 1 Black Tea Bag
  • 2 Black Cardamoms (Badi Elaichi)
  • 4-5 Cloves (Laung)
  • 2 Bay Leaves (Tej Patta)
  • 1-inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini)
  • Salt to taste
For Masala
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 2 large Onions, finely chopped (Pyaz)
  • 2-3 Green Chilies, slit (Hari Mirch)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • 3 large Tomatoes, finely chopped (Tamatar)
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1.5 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 2 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 2 tsp Cumin Powder (Jeera Powder)
  • 1.5 tsp Garam Masala
  • 2 tsp Dry Mango Powder (Amchur)
  • 1 tsp Pomegranate Seed Powder (Anardana Powder)
  • 1 tsp Black Salt (Kala Namak)
For Garnish
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves, chopped (Hara Dhaniya)
  • Lemon Wedges (Nimbu)
  • Sliced Onions (Pyaz)
Recipe :-
Step 1: Cook the Chole
  1. Wash and soak the chickpeas overnight.
  2. Drain and rinse the soaked chickpeas. Transfer to a pressure cooker with 4-5 cups of water.
  3. Add black tea bag, black cardamoms, cloves, bay leaves, cinnamon stick, and salt.
  4. Pressure cook for 20-25 minutes or until the chickpeas are soft and cooked through. Discard the tea bag and whole spices. Set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Masala
  1. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and the oil separates from the masala.
Step 3: Add Spices
  1. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
Step 4: Combine Chole and Masala
  1. Add the cooked chickpeas to the masala and mix well.
  2. Add garam masala, dry mango powder, pomegranate seed powder, and black salt. Mix thoroughly.
  3. Simmer the chole on low heat for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally to let the flavors meld.
Step 5: Finish and Serve
  1. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  2. Serve hot with lemon wedges, sliced onions, and bhatura, puri, or rice.
Enjoy your delicious Pindi Chole!
