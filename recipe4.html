Mutton Rogan Josh

Ingredients :-
For Marination
  • 1 kg Mutton (goat meat, cut into pieces)
  • 1 cup Yogurt (Dahi)
  • 2 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • Salt to taste
For Gravy
  • 3 large Onions, finely chopped (Pyaz)
  • 2 large Tomatoes, chopped (Tamatar)
  • 2 large Tomatoes, chopped (Tamatar)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Powder (Jeera Powder)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri Red Chili Powder (for color)
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1/2 cup Mustard Oil or Ghee
  • 2-3 Bay Leaves (Tej Patta)
  • 4-5 Cloves (Laung)
  • 2-3 Green Cardamoms (Hari Elaichi)
  • 1 Black Cardamom (Badi Elaichi)
  • 1-inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini)
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves for garnish (Hara Dhaniya)
Recipe :-
Step 1: Marinate the Mutton
  1. In a large bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt.
  2. Add the mutton pieces and coat them well with the marinade.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
Step 2: Prepare the Gravy
  1. Heat mustard oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat.
  2. Add bay leaves, cloves, green cardamoms, black cardamom, and cinnamon stick. Sauté until aromatic.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and oil separates from the masala.
Step 3: Cook the Mutton
  1. Add the marinated mutton to the pot and cook on medium heat until the mutton is browned.
  2. Add red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, and Kashmiri red chili powder. Mix well.
  3. Add water, stir, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until the mutton is tender and the gravy thickens. Stir occasionally.
Step 4: Garnish and Serve
  1. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  2. Serve hot with steamed rice, naan, or roti.
Enjoy your delicious Mutton Rogan Josh!
