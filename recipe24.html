Malai Kofta

Ingredients :-
For Kofta
  • 2 cups Paneer, grated (Cottage Cheese)
  • 1 cup Potatoes, boiled and mashed (Aloo)
  • 2 tbsp Cornstarch (Cornflour)
  • 2 Green Chilies, finely chopped (Hari Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Ginger, grated (Adrak)
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying
  • 2 tbsp Cashews, chopped (Kaju)
  • 2 tbsp Raisins (Kishmish)
For Gravy
  • 3 large Tomatoes, pureed (Tamatar)
  • 2 large Onions, finely chopped (Pyaz)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Cream (Malai)
  • 1/4 cup Cashew Nuts, soaked and ground to a paste (Kaju)
  • 2 tbsp Oil or Ghee
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 1 Bay Leaf (Tej Patta)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Powder (Jeera Powder)
  • 1/2 tsp Garam Masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves, chopped (Hara Dhaniya)
Recipe :-
Step 1: Prepare the Kofta
  1. In a large bowl, combine grated paneer, mashed potatoes, cornstarch, chopped green chilies, grated ginger, garam masala, and salt. Mix well to form a smooth dough.
  2. Divide the mixture into small portions and shape each portion into a ball. Stuff each ball with a few chopped cashews and raisins.
  3. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the koftas until golden brown and crispy. Drain on paper towels and set aside.
Step 2: Prepare the Gravy
  1. Heat oil or ghee in a large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds and bay leaf. Let them splutter.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  7. Add the cashew paste and cook for another 2-3 minutes, stirring continuously.
  8. Add fresh cream and mix well. Cook for another 2 minutes.
  9. eason with salt and garam masala.
Step 3: Combine Kofta and Gravy
  1. Add the fried koftas to the gravy. Simmer for 5-7 minutes on low heat, allowing the koftas to absorb the flavors of the gravy.
Step 4: Finish and Serve
  1. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  2. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
Enjoy your delicious Malai Kofta!
