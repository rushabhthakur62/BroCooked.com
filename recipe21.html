Rajma

Ingredients :-
For Rajma
  • 1 cup Red Kidney Beans (Rajma), soaked overnight
  • 4 cups Water
  • 2 large Onions, finely chopped (Pyaz)
  • 3 large Tomatoes, pureed (Tamatar)
  • 2-3 Green Chilies, slit (Hari Mirch)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste (Adrak-Lehsun ka Paste)
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder (Haldi)
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder (Lal Mirch)
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder (Dhaniya Powder)
  • 1 tsp Cumin Powder (Jeera Powder)
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Dry Mango Powder (Amchur)
  • 3 tbsp Oil
  • Fresh Coriander Leaves for garnish (Hara Dhaniya)
  • Salt to taste
For Tempering
  • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds (Jeera)
  • 1 Bay Leaf (Tej Patta)
  • 1-inch Cinnamon Stick (Dalchini)
  • 3-4 Cloves (Laung)
  • 2-3 Green Cardamom (Elaichi)
Step 1: Prepare the Rajma
  • Soak the red kidney beans (rajma) overnight in water.
  • Drain and rinse the soaked rajma. Transfer to a pressure cooker with 4 cups of water.
  • Pressure cook for 15-20 minutes or until the rajma is soft and cooked through. Set aside
Recipe :-
Step 2: Tempering
  1. Heat 3 tbsp oil in a large pan or kadai over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, and cardamom. Let them splutter.
Step 3: Prepare the Masala Base
  1. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the masala.
Step 4: Add Spices
  1. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
Step 5: Cook the Rajma
  1. Add the cooked rajma along with its water to the pan. Mix well.
  2. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer on low heat for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
Step 6: Finish and Serve
  1. Add garam masala and dry mango powder. Stir well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  2. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  3. Serve hot with steamed rice, jeera rice, or roti.
Enjoy your delicious Rajma!
